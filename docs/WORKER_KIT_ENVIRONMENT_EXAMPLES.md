# Worker Kit Environment Examples

This page maps the worker-kit packaging model to environment categories that Growthub can package.

## Creative Strategy

Current bundled example: `creative-strategist-v1`

Typical contents:

- strategy prompts
- planning templates
- example brand materials
- output standards for briefs and strategic deliverables

Typical outputs:

- strategic briefs
- campaign planning artifacts
- structured creative direction outputs

## Email Marketing

Potential packaged environment:

- campaign prompts
- sequence templates
- messaging examples
- QA and review standards

Typical outputs:

- email sequences
- campaign briefs
- draft messages
- review notes

## Motion / Remotion

Potential packaged environment:

- concepting prompts
- storyboard templates
- composition references
- output rules for motion artifacts
- local runtime assumptions for editor and browser workflows

Typical outputs:

- composition plans
- asset maps
- local machine video artifacts

The necessary local execution pattern already exists. Formal bundling is the remaining step.

## Browser-Heavy GTM

Potential packaged environment:

- browser research prompts
- prospecting templates
- workflow standards for sourcing, QA, or browser-assisted execution
- runtime assumptions about browser-capable adapters

Typical outputs:

- sourced lead lists
- enriched research
- QA notes
- browser-driven operational artifacts

## Research And Sourcing

Potential packaged environment:

- research prompts
- note-taking templates
- qualification examples
- evidence and output standards

Typical outputs:

- structured research packets
- sourcing lists
- prioritization notes

## Why These Examples Matter

The point is that the same worker-kit packaging model can support many specialized environments once they are:

- frozen
- documented
- validated
- bundled

This is why the docs treat worker kits as a primary packaging surface rather than a small CLI convenience.
